**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: Prospective Real Estate Buyer Confidentiality Agreement**

This document is a Confidentiality Agreement (NDA) between a Landlord/Landlord's Agent and a Prospective Purchaser/Purchaser's Agent. Its primary purpose is to allow the Prospective Purchaser to evaluate a potential property acquisition by receiving confidential information, while strictly protecting that information from unauthorized disclosure or misuse.

**Key Financial Obligations:**
This section of the contract does not contain any direct financial terms, payment amounts, fees, or pricing models. However, a breach of the confidentiality obligations could lead to significant financial liabilities for the Prospective Purchaser, as unauthorized disclosure is deemed to cause "substantial and irreparable harm" to the Landlord.

**Top Risks & Concerns:**
*   **One-Sided Liability & Waiver of Recourse:** The Landlord/Agent explicitly disclaim any warranty regarding the accuracy or completeness of the provided information. Critically, the Prospective Purchaser *expressly waives all rights of recourse* against the Landlord/Agent for any issues related to this information. This places the entire burden of verification and risk of misinformation solely on the Purchaser.
*   **Broad & Indefinite Confidentiality:** The definition of "Information" is very broad, covering all non-public data received now or in the future. The confidentiality obligation is perpetual, stated to "survive the terms of any listing agreement," which is highly burdensome and unusual for a standard NDA.
*   **Restricted Due Diligence:** The Prospective Purchaser is prohibited from directly contacting any persons concerning the Property (e.g., employees, suppliers, tenants) without the Landlord's written permission. This significantly hinders independent verification and thorough due diligence.
*   **High Bar for Breach Damages:** The agreement states that unauthorized disclosure, even without intent to harm, could cause "substantial and irreparable harm," setting a high potential liability for the Prospective Purchaser.

**Critical Dates & Deadlines:**
There are no specific start, end, milestone, or renewal dates for the agreement itself. The confidentiality obligation is indefinite, designed to "survive" other agreements, meaning it has no specified termination date.

**Overall Recommendation: Negotiate**
Given the significant one-sided risks, particularly the complete waiver of recourse against the Landlord for potentially inaccurate information and the perpetual confidentiality obligation, signing this agreement in its current form is not recommended. We should seek to negotiate the following:
*   **Limit Liability:** Introduce mutual representations regarding information accuracy or, at minimum, limit the Prospective Purchaser's waiver of recourse.
*   **Define Confidentiality Term:** Establish a reasonable, finite duration for the confidentiality obligation (e.g., 2-3 years).
*   **Enable Due Diligence:** Allow for reasonable direct contact with third parties for verification purposes, perhaps with appropriate notice or supervision.
*   **Clarify Termination:** Add clauses for the termination of the agreement itself.
